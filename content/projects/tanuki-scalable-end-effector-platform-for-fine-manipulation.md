## Project Overview

Tanuki is a modular end-effector platform designed to deliver the reliability and robustness of a simple robot gripper while achieving significantly higher manipulation performance.

The platform is built as a scalable standalone system with modular gripper and sensor components, enabling flexible robot manipulation workflows. It is designed for easy deployment and customization in both hardware and software, and supports automatic tool mounting for strong task versatility.

## Objectives

- Reliability and robustness: Maintain dependable operation comparable to a simple industrial robot gripper.
- Performance improvement: Deliver higher manipulation performance than a simple gripper baseline.
- Precision and speed: Achieve 100x more accurate motion and 15x faster motion.
- Dynamic control: Increase control bandwidth by 20x for responsive and stable manipulation.
- Control flexibility: Support both position-control and torque-control modes.
- Versatility and scalability: Enable broad task adaptability through modular architecture and automatic tool mounting.

## Role and Responsibilities

I contributed to the development and integration cycle of the Tanuki platform with a focus on design and electromechanical integration.

- Participated in design reviews for gripper architecture and subsystem integration decisions.
- Worked on power and electronics integration for the gripper system.
- Supported cable routing design and hardware packaging for robust deployment.
- Assisted with electical component selection, sourcing, fabrication, and electronics box assembly.
- Collaborated with cross-functional team members to improve deployment readiness and system robustness.

## Outcome
- Delivered a modular end-effector platform that remains robust while substantially improving manipulation performance.
- Demonstrated major gains in accuracy, speed, and control bandwidth over simple gripper setups.
- Established a scalable standalone architecture suitable for future sensor and tool extensions.
