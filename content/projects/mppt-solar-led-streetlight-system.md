## Project Overview

This freelance project focused on developing a Maximum Power Point Tracking (MPPT) solar cell system for LED streetlights.

The system includes a current controller for the LED driver and a buck-boost converter for charging the battery from a solar panel. The work progressed from prototype circuit implementation to PCB-based versions, with practical deployment constraints such as heat dissipation and weatherproof enclosure design.

## Technical Scope

- MPPT-based solar energy harvesting for LED streetlight operation.
- LED driver current control.
- Buck-boost conversion for battery charging from a solar panel.
- Prototype circuit and PCB-versioned circuit development.
- Field-minded enclosure and thermal management considerations.

## My Contribution

- PCB design and routing: Designed PCB layouts of an entire circuit.
- Component sourcing and vendor coordination: Coordinated inductor sourcing and supported procurement of key build components.
- Prototype and production build: Assembled prototype PCBs and contact vendors for fabricated V2 boards.
- Mechanical integration: Contributed enclosure dimensions, cover design support, and heat management for outdoor operation.
- Validation and reliability: Performed protection-circuit testing and final product verification.

## Outcome

- Delivered practical hardware iterations from prototype to finalized PCB versions.
- Supported performance and reliability improvements through enclosure/thermal decisions and protection testing.
- Helped transition the design toward deployable streetlight power-system products.
