## Project Overview

This project investigated the feasibility of using Femtoprint, a femtosecond-laser-based fabrication process, to create MEMS structures with complex 3D geometry and out-of-plane motion.

Traditional MEMS fabrication is typically constrained by planar lithography-driven processes, which makes vertically coupled motion and true 3D structural behavior difficult to realize. This work explored whether Femtoprint could open a more practical route to microdevices with non-planar compliance and displacement.

The project was carried out as both my bachelor's thesis at King Mongkut's University of Technology Thonburi and my internship work at FEMTO-ST Institute in Besancon, France.

## Research Context

The work focused on early-stage investigation and feasibility assessment of a new fabrication approach for micropositioning structures.

I designed simple MEMS concepts for initial prototyping, then extended the study toward more complex 3D-compliant micropositioner structures that would be difficult to achieve with conventional lithography-based microfabrication.

The more advanced concept study was developed into a conference paper on XZ MEMS micropositioning with 3D-complex structure and presented through the DTIP technical conference track.

## Objectives

- Evaluate the feasibility of Femtoprint as a fabrication route for complex MEMS structures.
- Investigate how 3D geometry can enable out-of-plane motion that is difficult to realize with conventional planar microfabrication.
- Develop candidate MEMS micropositioner concepts suitable for early prototyping and simulation.
- Compare manufacturability and structural behavior between simple demonstrators and more complex micropositioning designs.

## My Contribution

I led the investigation from concept definition to early validation support.

- Defined the technical direction for evaluating Femtoprint as a MEMS fabrication method for out-of-plane motion.
- Designed MEMS structures for feasibility study and coordinated with technicians on fabrication of initial simple prototypes.
- Performed structural simulation of more complex micropositioner concepts, with focus on stress and deformation behavior.
- Collaborated with FEMTO-ST technical staff to align designs with fabrication constraints and process capability.
- Consolidated the feasibility study into thesis work and publication material for conference presentation.

## Outcome

- Established practical understanding of how Femtoprint could support MEMS structures beyond conventional planar process limits.
- Delivered design concepts and initial prototype-oriented structures for fabrication assessment.
- Demonstrated, through simulation, the viability of more complex out-of-plane MEMS micropositioner architectures.
- Produced conference publication output connected to the thesis and internship work.
