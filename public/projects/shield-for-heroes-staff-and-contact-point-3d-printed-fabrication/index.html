Shield for Heroes: Staff and Contact-Point 3D-Printed Fabrication

Volunteer fabrication effort for 3D-printed face shields, supporting medical staff and rural hospitals across Thailand during the COVID-19 period.

Associated with: Volunteer | Project Date: 2020

Project Overview

This volunteer project focused on rapid, practical production of 3D-printed face shields for healthcare use.

Working with a small fabrication team, we produced face shield frames and supported assembly logistics so the final kits could be delivered to medical staff, including rural hospitals in Thailand.

Scope and Impact

  • Individually fabricated more than 300 pieces of 3D-printed face shields.
  • Facilitated a fabrication team of around 8 people by sharing CAD files, technical guidance, materials, and key parts.
  • Distribution of more than 1000 pieces of face shield in total to medical staff across Thailand.

My Contribution

  • Produced 3D-printed frames and prepared units for assembly.
  • Shared and maintained CAD files for repeatable and reliable fabrication.
  • Provided technical advice on printability, fit, and practical manufacturing constraints.
  • Supported sourcing and coordination of transparent sheets, cushion pads, and elastic bands.
  • Helped coordinate small-team execution to keep output and delivery consistent.

Outcome

  • Delivered high-volume, community-driven protective equipment support during urgent demand.
  • Enabled consistent production quality through shared technical resources and team coordination.
  • Contributed to direct support for frontline medical staff and rural healthcare facilities.
