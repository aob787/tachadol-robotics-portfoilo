Mobile Robot Prototype for Explosive Area

Designed and developed prototype mobile robot bases for operation in harsh and explosive environments.

Associated with: AI and Robotics Ventures (ARV) | Project Date: Nov 2020 - Aug 2021

Project Overview

This project focused on developing a mobile robot prototype for explosive-area operation, where robustness, safety, and reliable mechanical architecture are critical.

The work emphasized practical deployment constraints, including harsh operating conditions, enclosure constraints, and component-level thermal and reliability considerations.

Objectives

  • Design a mobile robot base prototype suitable for harsh and explosive environments.
  • Improve robustness and maintainability for real-world field usage.
  • Conduct fesibility analysis of component and enclosure for safe operation.

Role and Responsibilities

  • Designed and developed prototype mobile robot bases for explosive-area applications.
  • Coordinated with vendors for component sourcing and fabrication.
  • Performed thermal analysis for electrical components within enclosures.
  • Supported integration activities toward practical deployment readiness.

Outcome

  • Delivered a prototype mobile robot base architecture adapted to harsh/explosive use cases.
  • Improved design feasibility through vendor collaboration and thermal validation.
  • Contributed to ARV outcomes associated with PTTEP Innovation Gold/Silver award-winning results.
