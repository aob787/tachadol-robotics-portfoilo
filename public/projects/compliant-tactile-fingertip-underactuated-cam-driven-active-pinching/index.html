Compliant Tactile Fingertip with Underactuated Cam-Driven Active Pinching

A smart fingertip retrofit that uses underactuated cam-driven flexure to improve grasping of slippery and soft objects.

Project Overview

Standard industrial parallel jaw grippers often struggle with slippery and soft objects, such as food or biological tissue, because their motion is rigid and passive.

This project bridges the gap between simple grippers and complex dexterous hands by introducing a smart fingertip retrofit. The system uses an underactuated mechanism where the gripper closing motion drives a cam that flexes the fingertip, allowing it to wrap around objects for more secure and distributed contact.

Objectives

  • Mechanical innovation: Design a kinematic coupling (cam-shaft drivetrain) that converts jaw displacement into prescribed fingertip flexure without extra motors.
  • Sensing integration: Embed a flexible capacitive sensing matrix that keeps high sensitivity while the fingertip bends and protects the sensor from overload.
  • Surface engineering: Develop a bio-inspired dual-layer surface using food-safe materials for 3D sensing and friction control.
  • Functional validation: Demonstrate improved grasp success on challenging items such as raw salmon and cherry tomatoes versus rigid baseline grippers.

Role and Responsibilities

I executed the full development cycle for this robotic end-effector system.

  • Design and simulation: Performed mechanical design and simulation of cam profiles and flexure geometry for accurate kinematic coupling and structural reliability.
  • Prototyping: Fabricated modular fingertips, integrated flexible electronics, and cast bio-inspired surface textures using food-safe certified polymers.
  • Deployment: Shipped and integrated hardware across two laboratory environments with standard parallel jaw gripper interfaces.
  • Testing and characterization: Led experimental validation and characterized capacitive sensor spatial resolution across the full cam-driven bending range.
  • Iterative improvement: Analyzed failure modes in cooked bean and raw salmon trials and refined the cam profile for stronger grasp-and-release behavior.

Outcome

  • Increased dexterity by adding active compliance to existing hardware.
  • Stable tactile feedback across controlled bending angles for better force modulation.
  • Higher grasp success rate on deformable objects compared with plain-jaw baselines.
  • Fully manufacturable modular design compatible with industrial grippers without host robot hardware or software changes.
